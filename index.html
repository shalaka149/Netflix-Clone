Frequently Asked Questions

  • Netflix is a subscription-based streaming service that allows users to watch a wide variety of TV shows, movies, documentaries, and original content on internet-connected devices. Founded in 1997 as a DVD rental service, it has since evolved into one of the world’s leading entertainment platforms.

  • Netflix offers four subscription plans in India: Mobile (₹149), Basic (₹199), Standard (₹499), and Premium (₹649) per month. Plans vary by video quality (SD to 4K), number of devices, and download options. All plans are ad-free and provide unlimited access to Netflix content.

  • You can watch Netflix on a variety of internet-connected devices, including smartphones, tablets, smart TVs, laptops, desktops, streaming devices like Chromecast or Fire Stick, and gaming consoles such as PlayStation and Xbox. Just install the Netflix app or visit the website, sign in with your account, and start streaming your favorite content anytime, anywhere.

  • To cancel your Netflix subscription, go to netflix.com, sign in, click your profile icon, and select "Account." Under "Membership & Billing," click "Cancel Membership" and confirm. Your account stays active until the end of your billing cycle. If you signed up through a third party like Google Play or iTunes, cancel it there instead.

  • On Netflix, you can watch a wide variety of content including movies, TV shows, documentaries, anime, and Netflix Originals like Stranger Things, The Witcher, and Bridgerton. It features content across genres—action, comedy, drama, thriller, romance, and more—from both international and regional creators. New titles are added regularly to keep the library fresh and diverse.

  • Yes, Netflix is good for kids as it offers a dedicated Kids profile with age-appropriate content, parental controls, and content filters. This helps ensure children only access shows and movies suitable for their age group, providing a safe and fun viewing experience. Parents can also set PINs to restrict access to mature content.
